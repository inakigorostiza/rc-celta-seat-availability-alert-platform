# Templates & Resources

Reusable templates and frameworks for running Agent Team marketing campaigns.

## IE-Nergy Brief Template

**File**: `ienergy-brief-template.md`

The complete product brief for the IE-Nergy energy drink launch. Includes:
- Product overview and formulation
- Pricing and distribution strategy
- Target audience definition
- Key differentiator
- Launch goals and success metrics
- Available channels
- What NOT to say
- Team role assignments
- 6-week timeline

**When to use**: Copy this into your project folder as `brief.md` before spawning your team. All three teammates will read it as shared context.

**How to customize**: Replace IE-Nergy product details with your own product, keep the structure (audience, positioning, channels, guardrails).

## Team Roles Template

**File**: `team-roles-template.md`

Framework for defining specialist roles. Shows:
- **Strategist template** — With IE-Nergy example
- **Copywriter template** — With IE-Nergy example  
- **Researcher template** — With IE-Nergy example
- **Optional roles** — Campus Planner, Paid Media Strategist, Content Producer
- **Workflow patterns** — Sequential, debate, critique panel
- **Role definition checklist** — What makes a good role

**When to use**: When you want to customize your team for a different product or market. Copy the relevant role definition, adapt it for your brand, paste into your spawn prompt.

**Key insight**: Specific role definitions beat generic task descriptions. "You are a Gen Z direct-response copywriter who hates buzzwords and writes like a smart friend" produces sharper work than "write Instagram copy."

## Critique Checklist

**File**: `critique-checklist.md`

Detailed rubric for evaluating campaign deliverables. The most important template.

Includes weak vs. strong examples for:
- **Research** — Is this actionable insight or just feature comparison?
- **Strategy** — Is the positioning ownable or generic?
- **Instagram copy** — Does it sound like a person or a brand?
- **TikTok script** — Does it use TikTok's language or read like a TV spot?
- **Email** — Would you actually send this?
- **Search ads** — Is it differentiated or commodity?
- **Campaign summary** — Does the lead make a clear recommendation?

Plus:
- Overall campaign assessment checklist
- How to give feedback back to the team
- When to refine vs. start fresh

**When to use**: After your team finishes. Spend 15-20 minutes reviewing deliverables against this checklist. This develops your critical judgment.

[Download Critique Checklist →](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/igorostiza/labs/main/agent-teams-marketing-lab/templates/critique-checklist.md)

## How to Use These

### First campaign (IE-Nergy)

1. Copy the **IE-Nergy Brief** into your project folder as `brief.md`
2. Use the **Quick Reference Card** for tmux/troubleshooting
3. Use the **Critique Checklist** after the team finishes

### Second campaign (your product)

1. Adapt the **IE-Nergy Brief** to your product — keep the structure, change the details
2. Use **Team Roles Template** to define your teammates with the same specificity
3. Follow the same workflow from the [manual](manual.md)
4. Critique using the same checklist

### For instructors

All templates are designed to be customized:
- Swap the product details in the brief
- Adapt the role definitions for your market/audience
- The structure (sections, constraints, deliverables) stays the same

## Template Customization Checklist

When adapting these for a new product, make sure you define:

- [ ] **Product overview** — What is it? Why does it exist?
- [ ] **Target audience** — Be specific (not "young people" but "first-year CS students")
- [ ] **Positioning** — Core idea, not features
- [ ] **Differentiator** — What's NOT claimed? What won't we say?
- [ ] **Channels** — Where can we actually reach this audience?
- [ ] **Timeline** — Weeks until launch
- [ ] **Success metrics** — How will we know if it worked?
- [ ] **Team roles** — How should each specialist think about this?
- [ ] **Guardrails** — What specific phrases/approaches to avoid?

## Pro Tips

**Better briefs are sharper work.**
- Vague briefs get vague work, from humans and AI alike
- The more specific your product details, audience definition, and constraints, the better the output

**Constraints sharpen output.**
- "No buzzwords" is vague
- "Never use 'fuel,' 'crush,' 'unleash,' 'optimize,' 'unlock'" is actionable

**Specific voices beat generic tones.**
- "Write in a conversational tone" produces different output than "Write like you're explaining this to a friend at a coffee shop — specific, witty, no corporate speak"

**Show, don't describe.**
- Instead of describing the target audience, give an example: "first-year consultant working 60-hour weeks at a top-tier firm"
- Instead of describing tone, show a sentence: "The tone should be 'yeah, you know?' not 'please consider'"

**Define workflow.** Do teammates message each other? Who goes first? Who waits for whom?
