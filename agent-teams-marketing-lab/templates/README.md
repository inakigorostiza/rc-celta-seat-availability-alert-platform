# Templates & Resources

This folder contains reusable templates for running Agent Team marketing campaigns.

## Files

### ienergy-brief-template.md
The complete IE-Nergy launch brief — everything your team needs to know about the product, positioning, audience, channels, and constraints. Use this as your starting point for the exercise, or adapt it for your own product.

**When to use**: Before spawning your team. Print it or load it in the brief.md file that teammates will read.

### team-roles-template.md
Framework for defining specialist roles (Strategist, Copywriter, Researcher, plus optional roles like Campus Planner or Paid Media Strategist). Shows both the template form and working examples.

**When to use**: When you want to spawn a team for a different product or market. Copy the relevant role, customize it for your brand, and paste it into your spawn prompt.

### quick-reference-card.md
One-page cheat sheet with tmux commands, steering tips, troubleshooting, and a review checklist. Print or bookmark.

**When to use**: Keep this open in a browser tab or printed next to your monitor while running your first campaign.

### critique-checklist.md
Detailed rubric for evaluating campaign deliverables. Weak examples vs. strong examples for each piece (research, strategy, copy, synthesis). Teaches you what to look for when critiquing AI output.

**When to use**: After your team finishes, spend 15 minutes reviewing the deliverables against this checklist. This is where the real learning happens.

## How to Use These

### First time through (IE-Nergy exercise)

1. Read MANUAL.md in the parent directory (all of it)
2. Use **ienergy-brief-template.md** — copy it into your project folder as brief.md
3. Keep **quick-reference-card.md** handy for tmux/troubleshooting
4. Use **critique-checklist.md** after the team finishes

### Running a second campaign (your own product)

1. Adapt **ienergy-brief-template.md** to your product—same structure, your product facts
2. Use **team-roles-template.md** to define your teammates' roles with the same specificity
3. Follow the same workflow from MANUAL.md Part 4 onwards
4. Critique using the same checklist

### Customizing for your classroom or organization

- **For instructors**: The brief template and role template are designed to be customized. Change the product, market, timeline, and audience—the structure stays the same.
- **For teams**: Use the role definitions as job descriptions. The more specific you are in the brief, the better the output.

## Template Customization Checklist

When adapting these for a new product:

- [ ] Product overview — what is it? Why does it exist?
- [ ] Target audience — be specific (not "young people" but "students in first-year CS programs")
- [ ] Positioning — what's the core idea? (Not features.)
- [ ] Differentiator — what's NOT claimed? What do competitors say that we won't?
- [ ] Channels — which platforms can we actually reach our audience on?
- [ ] Timeline — how much time until launch?
- [ ] Success metrics — how will we know if it worked?
- [ ] Role definitions — how should each specialist think about this product?

## Questions?

**My team is producing generic copy.**
→ Check the role definition. Are you specific enough about voice and constraints? See team-roles-template.md for examples.

**I don't know how to critique the output.**
→ Read critique-checklist.md. Compare your team's work to the "weak" vs "strong" examples.

**I want to run this with my own product.**
→ Adapt ienergy-brief-template.md to your product, keeping the same structure and sections. The framework works for any launch campaign.

**How do I know if the positioning is good?**
→ See the "Is the positioning statement actually different?" section in critique-checklist.md. That's the key question.
